# PDF Templates Guide

This guide explains how to customize the appearance of your PDF invoices using the PDF Templates settings in Chrisnov Invoice.

## Accessing PDF Template Settings

1. Navigate to **Settings** from the main sidebar.
2. Click on the **PDF Templates** card.

## Customization Options

You can customize several aspects of your PDF invoices to match your brand identity.

### Template Style

Choose from a selection of professionally designed templates:

- **Professional**: A clean and corporate look with a blue color scheme. Ideal for formal business contexts.
- **Modern**: A fresh and contemporary design with a green color scheme.
- **Minimal**: A simple and elegant template with a grayscale palette, focusing on typography and clean lines.
- **Classic**: A stylish and traditional design featuring a purple color scheme.

### Header and Accent Colors

- **Header Color**: Customize the main color used in the invoice header. You can choose from a palette of pre-defined colors (Blue, Green, Purple, Gray, etc.) to align with your chosen template or brand.
- **Accent Color**: Select a color for secondary elements like borders and total amounts. This allows for further brand personalization.

### Logo Options

- **Logo Position**: Control the placement of your logo in the invoice header. Options include:
  - Left
  - Center
  - Right
- **Show/Hide Logo**: You can choose whether or not to include your logo on the invoices. If hidden, only your business name will be displayed.

### Footer Text

- **Custom Footer**: Define custom text to appear in the footer of every invoice. This is a great place for a thank you message, payment terms, or other important information.

## How to Customize Your Template

1. Go to the **PDF Templates** settings page.
2. Select a **Template Style** from the available options.
3. Choose your desired **Header Color** and **Accent Color**.
4. Set the **Logo Position** and toggle the logo's visibility.
5. Enter your custom **Footer Text**.
6. Click **Save Changes**.

All newly generated PDFs will now use your customized settings.
